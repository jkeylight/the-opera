ACT I

THE ARRIVAL

The city disappears behind you.

Carriages depart.

Footsteps fade.

Rain evaporates into stone.

Only the Opera remains.

Golden light spills from towering windows.

Velvet curtains conceal impossible stories.

The doors open.

The performance has already begun.

ACT I

THE GRAND ENTRANCE

Every great story begins with an arrival.

Not because the arrival matters.

Because anticipation matters.

The Opera understands anticipation.

It was built upon anticipation.

Visitors enter beneath marble arches.

Whispers replace conversation.

Expectation replaces certainty.

ACT I

THE STAIRCASE

A staircase can be architecture.

Or it can be theatre.

This staircase chose theatre.

Each step elevates importance.

Each landing increases tension.

Each glance upward promises revelation.

Yet reveals nothing.

ACT II

THE FOYER

Gold reflects candlelight.

Crystal reflects gold.

Mirrors reflect everyone.

Except the truth.

The truth remains hidden.

Somewhere beyond the velvet curtains.

ACT II

THE MIRROR GALLERY

People come here searching for themselves.

Instead they discover obsession.

Reflections multiply endlessly.

Reality becomes uncertain.

Memory becomes unreliable.

The Opera enjoys uncertainty.

ACT II

THE FIRST GLIMPSE

A silhouette.

Nothing more.

A movement behind silk curtains.

A figure crossing a distant balcony.

The audience stops breathing.

The moment vanishes.

The fascination remains.

ACT III

THE ROYAL BOXES

Power rarely sits on stage.

Power watches.

Power observes.

Power chooses.

The Royal Boxes were designed for this purpose.

From here empires have applauded.

Dynasties have whispered.

History has changed direction.

Tonight all attention moves elsewhere.

Toward Valentina Rossi.

ACT III

THE SALONS

The official performance continues.

Yet the real conversations happen here.

Behind closed doors.

Behind velvet walls.

Behind smiles.

The Opera has always understood this.

ACT III

THE RED CORRIDORS

Long corridors.

Endless velvet.

Soft light.

Quiet footsteps.

Every corridor feels familiar.

Even when it has never been seen before.

ACT IV

THE ORCHESTRA

Strings begin.

The room changes.

Wood vibrates.

Air vibrates.

Memory vibrates.

Music transforms architecture into emotion.

Emotion transforms audiences into believers.

ACT IV

THE WINGS

Every masterpiece contains hidden machinery.

The Opera is no different.

Behind elegance:

Precision.

Behind beauty:

Discipline.

Behind wonder:

Control.

ACT IV

THE DRESS CIRCLE

The audience watches the stage.

The stage watches the audience.

The relationship remains unresolved.

Until she appears.

ACT V

THE REVELATION

The room becomes still.

Conversation ends.

Movement ends.

Expectation reaches its limit.

Valentina Rossi arrives.

Nothing else changes.

Everything changes.

ACT V

THE ARIA

Some performances are remembered.

Some performances become legend.

This performance belongs elsewhere.

Beyond memory.

Beyond criticism.

Beyond explanation.

ACT V

THE GOLDEN HOUR

Light falls differently now.

Warmer.

Softer.

More forgiving.

The Opera itself appears enchanted.

ACT VI

THE DEVOTION

At first the audience observed.

Then admired.

Then followed.

Now they surrender.

The distinction matters.

Admiration is temporary.

Devotion is permanent.

ACT VI

THE FINAL MOVEMENT

The music grows quieter.

Not weaker.

More intimate.

More dangerous.

The room leans closer.

The Opera prepares its final act.

ACT VI

THE SILENCE

Silence is not emptiness.

Silence is anticipation without movement.

Silence is desire without resolution.

Silence is the final luxury.

ACT VII

THE FINAL CURTAIN

The curtain descends.

Slowly.

Inevitably.

Beautiful things end.

That is what makes them beautiful.

ACT VII

THE APPLAUSE

The audience rises.

History rises with them.

The room becomes thunder.

The applause refuses to end.

No one wishes to leave.

ACT VII

THE EMPTY STAGE

Eventually every theatre becomes quiet.

Every chandelier dims.

Every corridor empties.

Every note disappears.

Only memory remains.
